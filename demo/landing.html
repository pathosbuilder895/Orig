Three Steps to Academic Integrity

①

Build the Baseline

Student submits 3–5 early papers. Original extracts 103 stylometric features: sentence rhythm, vocabulary range, error patterns, argument structure, and more.

②

Analyze New Work

Every new submission is scored against the student's personal baseline using a quantum density matrix — a mathematical representation of their writing identity.

③

Flag Real Deviations

If the new work diverges statistically from their baseline, Original flags it — with a breakdown by tier showing which dimensions diverged and by how much.
