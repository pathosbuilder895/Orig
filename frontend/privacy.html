1. Overview & Our Commitment

Original is an authorship verification platform used by seminaries and colleges to detect potential academic integrity violations. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and similar data protection laws.

Our core commitment: Your writing belongs to you. We do not claim ownership of your essays, do not sell your writing, and do not use your work to train general-purpose AI models or language models.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Student Identity Information

  • Name — Your full name (for identification)
  • Student ID — Institutional student ID from your school
  • Email Address — Your school email (for notifications)
  • Course Information — Courses you're enrolled in

This data is collected from your school's system (Canvas, institutional SIS, or manual upload by your instructor).

2.2 Your Writing Submissions

  • Essay Text — The full text of assignments you submit
  • Submission Metadata — Assignment title, submission date, word count
  • Baseline Writing Samples — Authorized samples of your writing (proctored exams, verified essays)

2.3 Derived Data (Feature Vectors)

  • Stylometric Features — Statistical patterns from your writing (vocabulary, sentence structure, punctuation habits)
  • Authorship Signature — A mathematical profile of your unique writing style
  • Confidence Scores — How confident Original is in recognizing your writing

These features cannot be reverse-engineered to reconstruct your original text. They are similar to a fingerprint of your writing style.

3. How We Use Your Data

3.1 Primary Purpose: Authorship Verification

Original uses your data only to:

  • Build a profile of your unique writing style (based on baseline samples)
  • Compare future submissions against your profile
  • Flag submissions that deviate significantly from your known style (potential AI generation, plagiarism, or contract cheating)
  • Generate reports for your instructors to support academic integrity reviews

3.2 Legitimate Educational Interest

Under FERPA, your school has designated Original as a "school official" with legitimate educational interest in your academic records. We process your data on behalf of your institution solely for the purpose of supporting their academic integrity mission.

3.3 Institutional Reporting

We may provide your institution with aggregate reports showing:

  • Number of submissions analyzed
  • Percentage of submissions flagged
  • Model accuracy metrics
  • Trends in flagging rates

These reports do not identify individual students.

4. What We Do NOT Do With Your Data

We explicitly DO NOT:

  • ✗ Claim ownership of your writing or essays
  • ✗ Sell your writing or personal data to third parties
  • ✗ Use your writing to train AI models, language models, or machine learning systems (except to improve authorship detection itself)
  • ✗ Create a "corpus" or database of student writing for comparative analysis without consent
  • ✗ Share your data with law enforcement without a court order
  • ✗ Use your data for marketing, advertising, or commercial purposes
  • ✗ Combine your data with data from other students to create profiles or rankings
  • ✗ Sell your contact information to recruiters or other vendors

5. Data Retention & Deletion

5.1 Default Retention Period

Original retains your data as follows:

  • Active Period: While you are enrolled at your institution
  • Retention: One (1) year after your last submission or after you leave the institution
  • Automatic Deletion: After one year, your data is automatically deleted

5.2 Your Right to Request Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your data at any time. To request deletion:

  1. Contact your institution's data privacy officer or registrar
  2. Submit a written request asking for deletion of your Original account data
  3. Your institution will forward your request to Original
  4. Original will delete your data within 30 days

5.3 What Gets Deleted

When you request deletion, we delete:

  • All submission text and essay content
  • All baseline writing samples
  • Your authorship profile and feature vectors
  • All scoring results and flagging decisions
  • Any personal information associated with your account

5.4 Legal Holds & Exceptions

Original may retain your data longer if required by law, regulation, or a legal hold (e.g., if a flagged submission is under investigation). In such cases, we will inform you or your institution.

6. Security & Protection

6.1 Encryption

  • At Rest: All student data encrypted using AES-256-GCM (military-grade encryption)
  • In Transit: All network communication encrypted using TLS 1.3
  • Keys: Encryption keys are managed securely and rotated annually

6.2 Access Control

  • Role-Based: Only authorized people (your instructors, institution admins) can access your data
  • Students: Can only view their own data
  • Teachers: Can only view students in their courses
  • Admins: Can only view students in their institution

6.3 Audit Logging

All access to student data is logged. We maintain a record of:

  • Who accessed your data
  • When it was accessed
  • What action was taken
  • From which IP address

These logs are encrypted and retained for 2 years.

6.4 Vulnerability Management

  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Dependency scanning for known vulnerabilities
  • Security updates applied within 24 hours of release
  • SOC 2 Type II certification (annual audit)

7. Your Rights

7.1 Right to Access

You have the right to request access to all data Original holds about you. Submit a request to your institution's data privacy officer, and we will provide your data in a structured, portable format within 30 days.

7.2 Right to Correction

If any of your personal information is inaccurate (name, student ID, email), you can request correction. Contact your institution to submit a correction request.

7.3 Right to Deletion (Right to Be Forgotten)

Subject to legal holds and retention requirements, you can request that Original delete your data (see Section 5.2).

7.4 Right to Portability

You have the right to receive your data in a portable format (CSV, JSON, XML). Submit a request to your institution's data privacy officer.

7.5 Right to Opt-Out

If your institution permits, you may be able to opt out of Original's authorship verification system. Speak with your registrar or data privacy officer about opt-out options.

9. Breach Notification

9.1 Our Commitment

If Original experiences a data breach or unauthorized access to your data, we will:

  1. Notify your institution within 48 hours of discovery
  2. Provide details of what data was accessed
  3. Explain the steps we're taking to mitigate the breach
  4. Recommend actions your institution should take

9.2 Investigation & Mitigation

Upon discovering a breach, Original will:

  • Immediately isolate affected systems
  • Conduct a forensic investigation
  • Engage law enforcement if applicable
  • Implement additional security controls
  • Provide breach notifications as required by law
